Gear Needed for Rock Climbing

Gear will change depending on the type of climbing you're doing, but here's a good place to start for both bouldering and rope climbing: Quick Tip, DO NOT BUY USED LIFE SAVING GEAR Ex: ropes, harnesses, belay devices, quick draws, etc.

Bouldering

  1. Shoes
  2. Chalk
  3. Brush (optional but helpful)
  4. Crash Pads (outside)

Sport Climbing AKA Lead Climbing

  1. Shoes
  2. Chalk
  3. Harness
  4. Belay Device
  5. Rope
  6. Quick Draws (ouside)
  7. Personal Anchor System (ouside)
  8. Brush (optional but helpful)
PAS (Personal Anchor System)
A piece of climbing gear such as a sewn chain of slings that allows a climber to create a secure, independent connection between their harness and a climbing anchor. This is used for various situations such as cleaning routes by removing gear, or establishing an anchor during multi-pitch climbing.
Brush
There are different kinds of brushes but the most common you'll see look similar to a tooth brush, or a large version of that. Brushes are used to remove loose chalk and debris from holds. In short, it makes it easier to hold onto.
Quick Draws
A quick draw is two carabiners connected by a sewn sling (dogbone) used by sport climbers to attach the rope to protection points on a route, such as bolts.

Top Roping

  1. Shoes
  2. Chalk
  3. Harness
  4. Belay Device
  5. Rope (outside)
  6. Brush (optional but helpful)

Visit your local gear shop and discuss what gear will work best for you with someone knowledgeable.
