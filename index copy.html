Different Kinds of Rock Climbing

There are many different kinds of climbing, but here are the basics:

Aid VS Free Climbing

Free climbing does not mean climbing without a rope. That would be free soloing, the high-stakes activity made famous by Alex Honnold in his film, Free Solo. Rather, free climbing means the climber is simply trying to reach the top of his or her objective using only their physical bodies.

Safety systems such as a rope or bouldering pad may be used in the event of a fall, but not to aid upward progress. In contrast, aid climbing means the climber uses a variety of technical gear to painstakingly pull themselves up the cliff.

Bouldering

Bouldering is climbing without a rope on small cliffs and boulders where it is possible to land relatively safely, first evolved on the boulders of Fontainbleau outside of Paris in the mid-century. Because bouldering allows the climber to practice intricate movements unencumbered by a rope or safety concerns, it is the premier venue for pushing the absolute limits of free climbing.

Sport Climbing AKA Lead Climbing

Sport climbing is where routes are pre-bolted with permanent anchors for safety, and climbers use quickdraws to attach their rope to the bolts as they ascend. Sport climbing is more of an umbrella term as it refers to different kinds of climbing, but sport and lead climbing are often used interchangeably.

Top Roping

Top roping is a type of climbing where the rope is already fixed to the top of the route. The climber climbs while their belayer takes up the rope's slack as they ascend.
